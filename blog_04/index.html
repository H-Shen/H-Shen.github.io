Frequency of the Mode

Problem Description

In the classroom of Intelligence Specialized Training students, to liven up the atmosphere and reinforce the knowledge of bitwise operations, the students started playing a game.

There are students in the class, and each student has two cards, a red card or a black card. Each card has a non-negative integer that does not exceed . The red card number of the -th student is , and the black card number is .

Now, each student needs to play a card. Each student can either directly play the number on their red card or play the result of the bitwise XOR operation between their red and black card numbers. Finally, the teacher will collect all the numbers played by the students.

The number that appears the most among these numbers is the mode. With the best cooperative strategy for all students, we hope that the frequency of the mode is as high as possible. What is the number that appears the most?

Explanation

The mode can appear at most times, and there are two ways to achieve this:

  • The 1st student plays the red card directly, the 2nd student plays the XOR of red and black, the 3rd student plays any card, and the 4th student plays the XOR of red and black. In this way, students 1, 2, and 4 can all play .
  • The 1st student plays the XOR of red and black, the 2nd student plays the red card directly, the 3rd student plays the red card directly, and the 4th student plays any card. In this way, students 1, 2, and 3 can all play .

Therefore, both and are the modes that appear the most, as they can appear up to times, and there is no scheme where they appear times. However, since the smaller one should be output if there are multiple solutions, please output .

Solution

  • Problem Analysis

    To find the mode, we first need to count all possible card-playing scenarios for each student and calculate the frequency of each scenario. The smallest value among those with the highest frequency is what we are looking for. Since each student may play a red card or the XOR of two cards, we can handle this in two sub-cases:

    • If the red card played by the student and the XOR of two cards are different, they need to be counted separately.

    • If the red card played by the student and the XOR of two cards are the same, it is counted only once.

    Therefore:

    • To achieve fast access and update of frequencies during counting, a hash table is used for storage.
      The implementation of the hash table can use std::unordered_map or __gnu_pbds::gp_hash_table, with the latter being used here.

    • To prevent the XOR value from exceeding the int range, long long is used.

    • To reduce the basic operation time complexity of the hash table to
      O(n) in the case of special inputs, Codeforces’ anti-hack hash function is used, referenced from https://codeforces.com/blog/entry/62393.

    • To find the smallest value among those with the highest frequency, a linear check is needed after calculating the maximum frequency.

    • To avoid potential constant optimization issues, fast input and output can be added.

  • The code below is compiled using the C++17 standard and O2 optimization.
